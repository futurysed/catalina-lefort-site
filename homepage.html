Questions

Things people want
to know before booking.

Do I need to be vegan to work with you?

Not at all. You can eat meat now and that's completely fine. My approach is about helping you add more plants at a pace that works for you — not removing everything overnight. We work with where you are, not where you're "supposed" to be.

I'm scared I'll be deficient in B12 or protein. What do you say?

This is the most common fear — and it's completely valid. Here's the truth: you can get most nutrients from plants if you plan your meals. The only thing most people need to supplement is B12. We'll check your labs and I'll tell you exactly what applies to your body. No guessing.

How is this different from seeing a regular nutritionist?

I'm a medical doctor — which means I can read and interpret your labs, understand how your medications interact with your diet, and prescribe when needed. A nutritionist can give you a great meal plan. I give you a full medical picture and a plan that fits it.

Can you prescribe medication if I need it?

Yes. Lifestyle medicine is not "instead of medication" — it's alongside it when needed. If your labs show something that requires treatment, I'll prescribe. The goal is always to use lifestyle changes to reduce dependency on medication over time, not to ignore medical reality.

I don't know where to start. Is that okay?

That's exactly the right place to be. The first session is built for this. You don't need to arrive with a plan — I build it with you. All I need from you is honesty about where you are right now.

Where are you based? Do I need to be in Chile?

I'm based in Chile, but sessions are 100% online. You can book from anywhere in the world. Sessions are conducted in Spanish.
